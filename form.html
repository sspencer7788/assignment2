Wood Calculator

Below is a handy guide to figuring out approximate cost and how much wood you need to buy for the entire project. If you play around with the types of wood, you will find huge disparities in cost. My suggestion is to use Pine at first, for practice, before moving to the more expensive woods. Pine (or any softwood) is generally cheaper and easy to work with, making it the perfect medium to get the process down before dropping some real money on the nicer stuff. The form is a rough estimate, and you may find a wide disparity between your local prices, and this calculator. Lastly, remember when you take the total board lenth you will need, it will not account for cuts. You will need more to account for cut-offs and scrap. When you shop for the wood, measure each piece against the board you intend to use for that piece, to ensure you don't have to take the extra trip of shame to the home improvement store of your choice.
